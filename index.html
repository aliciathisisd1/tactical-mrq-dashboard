Mashreq UAE

February 2026 Performance

Mashreq UAE had a mixed February — LinkedIn engagements surged +157% driven by high-impact appointment posts, and the Ramadan Facebook reel (2,231 eng, 458.9K reach) was the top cross-platform performer. Overall engagements were down -23% MoM due to TikTok registering zero interactions and YouTube remaining flat, masking the genuine strength in LinkedIn and Facebook organic content.

🎯 Actions for March
  • Double down on: LinkedIn leadership and appointment posts — consistently driving 500–1,100 eng per post, outperforming all other formats.
  • Lean into: Ramadan video content on Facebook — the seasonal reel hit 2,231 eng and 458K reach, the best single post of the month.
  • Pull back on: unoptimised TikTok publishing — zero engagements recorded in February; pause and rethink format before resuming.

💡 What to apply to Mashreq Egypt
1
One emotion-led brand video per month can drive outsized reach. Banque Misr's Ramadan anthem hit 394K eng and 997K reach organically. Mashreq Egypt's content is product-led and performing well — adding one culturally resonant, non-product video per month could unlock a step-change in reach.
2
Video volume is the market's primary engagement driver. National Bank of Egypt generated 604K eng from Video + Reels combined. Mashreq Egypt is already moving this way (55K from video/reels) but the gap is 10x — increasing video cadence is the single highest-leverage action available.
3
Teaser posts generate massive earned reach at zero cost. NBE's "watch this space" Instagram post drove 115K eng and 48M reach with no product information. A pre-launch teaser series ahead of Ramadan campaigns or new product moments is an untested, low-effort format for Mashreq Egypt.

💡 What to apply to Mashreq Pakistan
1
Brand storytelling outperforms product posts. HBL's top two posts — a brand momentum manifesto and a brand identity refresh — drove 19K and 16K eng respectively with no product mention.
